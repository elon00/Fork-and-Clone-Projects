Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our URL shortener service.

URL shortening works by taking a long URL and creating a shorter, unique URL that redirects to the original URL. When someone clicks on the shortened link, they are first directed to our server, which then redirects them to the original destination. This process happens almost instantly and allows us to track clicks and gather analytics.

Yes, our shortened links are permanent by default. However, you can set an expiration date for your links if you want them to stop working after a certain time. Free users have links that expire after 30 days of inactivity, while paid plans offer permanent links with optional expiration settings.

Yes, with our Pro and Business plans, you can customize the back-half of your shortened URLs to make them more memorable and branded. For example, instead of "linkbr.ef/a1b2c3", you could have "linkbr.ef/summer-sale". Business plan users can also use their own custom domain for branded links.

Our analytics provide insights into how your links are performing. You can see the number of clicks, geographic location of clickers, referral sources, devices, browsers, and time of clicks. Pro and Business plans offer more detailed analytics and longer data retention periods.

Yes, we offer a RESTful API for Business plan users. The API allows you to create, manage, and retrieve analytics for your shortened URLs programmatically. This is perfect for integrating our service with your existing workflows and applications.
